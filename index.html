WO2021165412A2 - Workpiece Holding Devices and Associated Methods

Issued Aug 26, 2021
In the manufacturing industry, various manufacturing and assembly operations are performed on numerously configured workpieces. Such operations not only involve manufacturing and assembly operations being performed on the workpieces, but such operations also require handling and shuttling the workpieces between workstations. To properly hold the workpiece, tooling assemblies must be able to properly grasp and manipulate the workpiece. Tooling systems for grasping known or similar types of objects present fewer design problems in that a gripper design may be selected that is well suited to complete a particular task. In such instances, the grippers may include a pair of fingers having simple geometries, such as flat tips for engaging the workpiece. Alternatively, the fingertips may have customized geometries for engaging workpieces with specific geometries.

2022 - Norgren Adaptix Quick Change Fingertips

Unable to share images as this project is not yet released, current tip design shown. Users have the ability to change the "fingertips" of their Adaptix to change step height, add different studs, replace a tip and/or stud due to wear, etc. I am introducing a new design that makes use of a simpler mechanism, adding value to both the customer and our own internal assembly while eliminating several expensive components. Design in Inventor and analysis in Nastran.

2022 - Norgren Adaptix Housing Expansion

Led design effort to expand Adaptix compatibility to a larger range of vise brands and vise types. Produced 5 new housing designs while overseeing/approving the creation of 10 others. Design conducted in Inventor with careful tolerance analysis on each design to maintain high part accuracy. Conducted additional vibrational testing (pictured) to verify new flexible mount system.

2020/21 - IMI Norgren Rockford RMAs

IMI Rockford was struggling with very high return rates following a site consolidation. I led a 6 month project to improve the return and RMA process, overhaul the RMA assembly cell (pictured), implement new tracking metrics, and reduce quality issues at the source. I was able to reduce total RMA count, RMA process time, and RMA generation all by >50% while also improving several upstream assembly processes to prevent our most common return reasons.

2018 - Continuous Casting Water Model

Capstone senior design project at Colorado School of Mines. For the on-campus Continuous Casting Center, team delivered a large, complex water model to simulate the process of casting steel and allow testing of argon bubbling, variable tundish/stopper geometries, and various mold sizes. I served as both project manager and mechanical design lead. Component design in SolidWorks, parts made by hand mill and lathe machining, and pneumatic control implemented for stopper rod position. Delivered to CCC lab for use in steel industry research.

2016 - Bike Rental Station

Project at Colorado School of Mines. Design was for a proposed bike rental program on campus, allowing students to rent bikes with their student IDs. Students could also enter their ID number and rent bikes with pre-purchased credits. Prototype was done with an Arduino in two weeks with a num-pad, RFID scanner, display screen, and locking system. System was fully functional, scanning details from Mines issued student IDs. All code in Arduino C.

2016 - Crash Alert Device

Project at Colorado School of Mines. What happens when you are enjoying the Colorado outdoors on your bike and the unexpected happens? The crash alert device senses when a crash has occured and sends a text to an emergency contact with your location. The user can also indicate that they are ok to the same contact. Built with an Arduino and coded in Arduino C and Matlab. Quick 3D-printed housing.
